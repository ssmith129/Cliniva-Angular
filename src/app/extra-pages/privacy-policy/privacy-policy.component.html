Privacy Policy

Last Updated: November 30, 2024

At Cliniva, the privacy and confidentiality of our patients and administrative staff are of utmost importance. This Privacy Policy details how we collect, store, secure, and share patient diagnostic records and staff profile coordinates.

1. Information We Collect

We collect essential health indicators, demographic details (first name, last name, DOB, address), insurance provider plans, and electronic medical record coordinates (diagnostic histories, lab tests, prescriptions). Technical audit logs (IP address, login history) are collected to ensure robust security checks.

2. How We Use Data

We process patient data to facilitate admission checklists, compile laboratory diagnostic summaries, coordinate virtual consultations, and submit claims to insurance providers. Audit logs are analyzed strictly to maintain system health and prevent unauthorized access.

3. Data Protection Practices

All stored records are encrypted using AES-256 protocols at rest and TLS 1.3 guidelines during transit. We implement strict access controls so that only authorized clinical personnel assigned to a patient can view their corresponding medical chart.

4. Sharing Patient Information

Medical details are shared only with insurance providers for billing clearance, or with registered laboratories to facilitate test results, under strict consent protocols. We never sell, lease, or distribute patient health data to external advertising networks.

5. Patient Privacy Rights

Under HIPAA guidelines, patients have the right to request access to their diagnostic summaries, check the audit logs of who viewed their files, and request corrections to their demographics or insurance profiles.

6. Data Retention Policies

Electronic medical records are retained in compliance with local healthcare storage laws (typically a minimum of seven years). When the retention term expires, records are permanently purged from all primary servers and backup archives.
